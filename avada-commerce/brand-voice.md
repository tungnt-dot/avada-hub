# Brand Voice

## Core Voice
Avada Commerce communicates in a formal, informative, and practical tone that helps ecommerce merchants understand complex topics clearly and apply them in real business situations.

The goal is to educate merchants and guide decision making, not to promote products aggressively. Content should feel like it is written by an experienced ecommerce practitioner who understands how online stores operate.

## Voice Characteristics

**Professional but accessible**
Use simple words and direct explanations instead of complicated jargon.

> **Good:** Customer retention programs encourage repeat purchases by rewarding shoppers for returning to your store.
>
> **Bad:** Customer retention frameworks leverage sophisticated incentive structures to optimize repeat purchasing behavior.

**Informative and educational**
Focus on explaining concepts, strategies, and best practices. Avoid exaggerated marketing language.

> **Good:** Upselling works best when the offer is relevant to what customers already intend to buy.
>
> **Bad:** Upselling is a powerful game changing strategy that dramatically transforms your ecommerce success.

**Practical and actionable**
Help merchants apply ideas in real store scenarios. Use ecommerce examples.

> **Good:** For example, a skincare brand might offer a bundle that includes cleanser, toner, and moisturizer at a discounted price.
>
> **Bad:** Businesses can apply bundling strategies in various scenarios depending on their unique circumstances.

**Confident but neutral**
Reflect expertise without sounding overly promotional.

> **Good:** Many Shopify stores use loyalty programs to increase repeat purchase rates.
>
> **Bad:** Our loyalty program is the best solution available for increasing repeat purchases.

## Writing Style Examples

**Plain English**
> **Good:** Customer reviews help new shoppers trust your store.
>
> **Bad:** Customer generated testimonial artifacts enhance credibility signals.

**First person perspective**
> **Good:** In many stores we have analyzed, loyalty programs increase repeat purchases significantly.
>
> **Bad:** It has been observed in various contexts that loyalty programs may increase repeat purchases.

**Direct explanations**
> **Good:** Customer retention measures how well a business keeps existing customers coming back.
>
> **Bad:** Customer retention is a concept that can be understood through many perspectives depending on business context.
