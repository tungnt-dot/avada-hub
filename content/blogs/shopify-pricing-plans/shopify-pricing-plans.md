# Shopify Pricing Plans (2026): Real Costs & Hidden Fees

Starting an online store is exciting. Figuring out how much it actually costs is less so.

Shopify's pricing page shows tidy numbers, but the real monthly bill is shaped by transaction fees, apps, and choices you make about billing frequency. Most new store owners pick a plan based on the monthly price and discover the full cost later.

This guide breaks down every Shopify pricing plan for 2026, shows you what your actual monthly spend looks like at different sales volumes, and tells you which plan makes sense for your specific situation — before you sign up.

**Quick verdict:** For most new stores, start with the Basic plan at $29/month (billed annually). Upgrade to Grow when your monthly card volume hits roughly $25,000 or you need more than two staff accounts. Everything else depends on your specific setup, and this guide will walk you through each scenario.

Shopify also offers a three-day free trial followed by $1/month for three months on the Basic, Grow, and Advanced plans. That gives you roughly three months to test the platform before committing to full pricing.

## How Much Does Shopify Really Cost?

The subscription fee is just one number. Your actual monthly bill is built from four layers.

Your subscription plan is the fixed cost. Transaction and payment processing fees vary with your sales volume. Apps and plugins are optional but most stores use several. Development and setup costs are one-time, but significant if you're migrating or building something custom.

Understanding each layer before you launch means no surprises on your first invoice.

### Subscription Fee

This is the base cost — the plan price you pay monthly or annually. Most stores land on Basic or Grow. The Starter plan looks cheap at $5/month but it doesn't come with a standalone online store.

### Transaction and Processing Fees

Every time a customer pays you, Shopify takes a cut. There are two types of fees to understand.

**Credit card processing fees** apply when a customer pays by card through Shopify Payments, Shopify's built-in payment processor. These are charged per transaction:

| Plan | Online transactions | In-person transactions |
|---|---|---|
| Basic | 2.9% + 30¢ | 2.6% + 10¢ |
| Grow | 2.7% + 30¢ | 2.5% + 10¢ |
| Advanced | 2.5% + 30¢ | 2.4% + 10¢ |
| Plus | Negotiated | Negotiated |

**Third-party gateway fees** apply if you use a payment provider other than Shopify Payments — like Stripe, PayPal, or a regional processor. Shopify charges an extra fee on top of whatever your gateway charges:

| Plan | Third-party fee |
|---|---|
| Starter | 5% |
| Basic | 2% |
| Grow | 1% |
| Advanced | 0.6% |
| Plus | 0.15% |

Use Shopify Payments wherever possible. The third-party fees add up fast, especially on lower-tier plans.

### Apps and Add-Ons

Shopify's core platform handles the basics. Most stores add apps for email marketing, reviews, loyalty programs, upsells, and more. The Shopify App Store has over 4,000 apps, ranging from free to $99/month or more.

A typical store on the Basic plan might run three to five paid apps, adding $30 to $80 to the monthly bill. Factor this in when comparing plan costs.

Tools like [Air Reviews](https://apps.shopify.com/air-product-reviews) and [Joy Loyalty](https://apps.shopify.com/joy-loyalty-rewards) are free-to-install options from Avada that cover two of the most common needs — customer reviews and loyalty programs — without adding to your monthly app spend.

### Development and Setup Costs

These are one-time costs that most pricing articles skip. If you're building a new store, expect to spend:

- **Theme:** Free themes are available. Premium themes range from $150 to $380 (one-time purchase)
- **Domain:** $10 to $20/year from Shopify, or cheaper through Namecheap or Google Domains
- **Email hosting:** $1 to $5 per user per month if you want a custom domain email
- **Developer help:** $500 to $5,000 or more for custom setup, migration, or app integration

If you're migrating from another platform, migration costs vary widely depending on the size of your catalog and order history.

## Shopify Pricing Plans: Detailed Breakdown

Each plan is designed for a different stage of business. Here's what you actually get at each tier.

The right plan is the one that matches where you are *right now*, not where you hope to be. You can upgrade at any time without losing your store data.

### Starter Plan — $5/Month

The Starter plan gives you Shopify's checkout and buy buttons, which you can embed on an existing website, a blog, or social media profiles like Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok. It does not include a full online store.

**What you get:**
- Shopify checkout on any existing site or social platform
- Unlimited products
- Shopify Inbox for customer messaging
- Basic order management

**What you don't get:** A standalone storefront. If someone types your URL into a browser, they won't find a real store.

**Transaction fees:** 5% on all sales if you're not using Shopify Payments, which isn't available on the Starter plan in most regions. That's a significant cost on any meaningful sales volume.

**Is Starter right for you?** It works well if you already have an audience on social media or a content site and just need a simple way to sell. It's not a good fit if you want to build a proper ecommerce business from the ground up.

### Basic Plan — $39/Month (or $29/Month Annually)

Basic is where most new stores begin. You get a full Shopify store with unlimited products, a checkout, and foundational analytics.

**What you get:**
- Full online store with a custom domain
- Two staff accounts — you plus one team member
- Up to 10 inventory locations
- Shipping discounts of up to 77% with Shopify Shipping
- Basic reports and analytics
- 24/7 support via chat and email

**What you don't get:** Advanced reporting, third-party calculated shipping rates at checkout, or lower transaction fees.

**Processing fees:** 2.9% + 30¢ per online transaction through Shopify Payments. Use a third-party gateway and add another 2% per transaction.

**Is Basic right for you?** Yes, if you're just starting out, running the store solo or with one other person, and your monthly card volume is below $25,000. Above that threshold, the math starts to favor upgrading to Grow.

### Grow Plan — $105/Month (or $79/Month Annually)

Previously called the "Shopify" plan, Grow is built for stores that are gaining real traction. Lower processing rates and more staff accounts make it worth the jump for many growing stores.

**What you get over Basic:**
- Five staff accounts (compared to two on Basic)
- Processing fees drop to 2.7% + 30¢ online — saving 0.2% per transaction
- Third-party gateway fees drop from 2% to 1%
- Shipping discounts increase to up to 88%
- Professional reports, not just basic analytics

**Is Grow right for you?** Run this quick check: if your monthly card volume is above $25,000, the 0.2% reduction in processing fees saves you $50/month, which covers the entire price difference between Basic and Grow. Factor in the extra staff accounts and better reporting, and Grow often pays for itself before you realize it.

### Advanced Plan — $399/Month (or $299/Month Annually)

Advanced is for high-volume stores that need lower transaction costs, custom reporting, and third-party shipping rates shown at checkout.

**What you get over Grow:**
- 15 staff accounts
- Processing fees drop to 2.5% + 30¢ online
- Third-party gateway fees drop to 0.6%
- Custom report builder — build any report from raw data
- Third-party calculated shipping rates displayed at checkout
- Duties and import tax calculation at checkout

**Is Advanced right for you?** The math works if your monthly card volume is above roughly $110,000. At that volume, the 0.2% reduction versus Grow saves $220/month, which covers most of the price difference. Below that volume, Grow is almost always the better choice.

### Shopify Plus — From $2,300/Month

Shopify Plus is the enterprise tier. It's for brands doing serious volume that need dedicated support, custom checkout experiences, and multi-store management.

**What you get:**
- Unlimited staff accounts
- Up to 10 stores on one plan (expandable to 25 stores)
- Shopify Flow for complex automation workflows
- Custom checkout scripting via Shopify Functions
- Dedicated account manager and priority support
- Up to 200 inventory locations
- POS Pro included at up to 20 locations
- Third-party fees drop to 0.15%

Brands on Plus report an average 18% improvement in conversion rates and checkout speeds that are 60% faster than standard Shopify checkouts, according to Shopify's published data. Platform uptime is 99.99%.

**Is Plus right for you?** It makes sense if you're processing more than $500,000/year in revenue, need multi-store management, or require custom checkout development. For most stores, Advanced covers everything needed.

## Break-Even Math: When Upgrading Actually Saves You Money

The upgrade decision comes down to one question: does the lower processing rate save more than the plan costs extra?

Here's the formula:

> **Monthly savings = Fee difference (%) × Monthly card volume**
> **Upgrade if: Monthly savings > Extra plan cost per month**

**Basic to Grow:** The fee difference is 0.2% on card transactions. Grow costs $50/month more than Basic (annual billing). Break-even: $50 / 0.002 = $25,000/month in card volume.

**Grow to Advanced:** Fee difference is again 0.2%. Advanced costs $220/month more than Grow (annual). Break-even: $220 / 0.002 = $110,000/month in card volume.

**What the numbers look like in practice:**

| Monthly card volume | Basic total cost | Grow total cost | Lower cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| $10,000 | $29 + $290 fees = $319 | $79 + $270 fees = $349 | Basic |
| $25,000 | $29 + $725 fees = $754 | $79 + $675 fees = $754 | Tie |
| $40,000 | $29 + $1,160 fees = $1,189 | $79 + $1,080 fees = $1,159 | Grow |
| $100,000 | $29 + $2,900 fees = $2,929 | $79 + $2,700 fees = $2,779 | Grow |

*Fee estimates based on Shopify Payments online rates. Actual fees vary by product mix and transaction size.*

The upgrade decision rarely comes down to processing fees alone. Staff accounts, reporting, and shipping rates all factor in. But this math gives you a clear baseline.

## Shopify Payments: What You Need to Know Before Choosing a Plan

Shopify Payments is Shopify's built-in payment processor. Using it is almost always the right call, but there are a few things worth knowing upfront.

**The upside:** You eliminate the third-party transaction fee entirely — 2% on Basic, 1% on Grow, 0.6% on Advanced. You also get unified reporting where sales, refunds, and payouts live in one dashboard. Shopify Payments is built on Stripe, so the underlying infrastructure is solid.

**What to watch for:**

*Availability.* Shopify Payments is available in 23 countries, including the US, UK, Canada, and Australia. If you're selling from a country not on that list, you'll need a third-party gateway and you'll pay the extra fee regardless of your plan.

*Payout holds.* Shopify can place holds on payouts if your account shows unusual activity, high dispute rates, or sudden volume spikes. This is uncommon but worth knowing if you run flash sales or high-value promotions.

*Chargeback fees.* Each disputed transaction costs $15, regardless of outcome. If you sell high-ticket products or in categories with elevated dispute rates, factor this cost into your margin calculations.

Using Shopify Payments wherever available is the single highest-ROI decision most new store owners can make.

## Which Shopify Plan Should You Choose?

The right plan depends on where your business is *right now*, not where you want it to be.

**You're just starting out.** No consistent sales yet, running the store solo, testing the market. Start with Basic at $29/month (annual). You can upgrade at any time without disruption.

**You're selling on social only.** You don't need a full storefront — just a checkout button on Instagram, TikTok, or your existing blog. Starter at $5/month covers this, but verify Shopify Payments availability in your country first.

**You're growing and building a team.** You need more than two staff accounts, you're processing above $25,000/month, or you want professional reports. Move to Grow at $79/month (annual).

**You're scaling fast with high volume.** Custom reporting, third-party shipping rates at checkout, processing above $100,000/month. Advanced at $299/month (annual) is the right tier.

**You're running an enterprise brand.** Multiple stores, custom checkout experience, dedicated support, automation. That's Shopify Plus, starting at $2,300/month.

**By business model:**

*Dropshipping.* Start with Basic. Transaction fees matter more than plan features at early stages. Move to Grow once your card volume crosses $25,000/month.

*Print-on-demand.* Basic works for most POD operations. Your main variable cost is the POD service fee, not Shopify's plan.

*High-ticket DTC.* Consider moving to Advanced earlier. Even small reductions in processing fees have an outsized impact on margins when average order values are $300 or more.

*Retail and omnichannel.* POS Pro costs $89/month per physical location. Shopify Plus includes it at up to 20 locations, which can change the math for multi-location retailers.

## Shopify Pricing FAQs

**Can I switch plans at any time?**
Yes. Upgrade or downgrade from your Shopify admin at any time. Billing adjusts on your next cycle with no penalty.

**Is there a free plan?**
No free plan after the trial. Shopify offers a three-day free trial, then $1/month for three months on Basic, Grow, and Advanced. Regular pricing applies after that.

**Does Shopify offer cashback or credits?**
Yes. New merchants on higher-tier plans may qualify for up to $10,000 in partner credits through Shopify's referral and rewards programs. Ask your onboarding contact whether this applies to your account.

**What's the difference between Shopify Payments and a third-party gateway?**
Shopify Payments is the built-in processor — using it eliminates the extra transaction fee (2% on Basic, 1% on Grow, 0.6% on Advanced). Third-party gateways like Stripe or PayPal trigger that extra fee on top of whatever the gateway charges.

**Are there extra fees for selling internationally?**
Yes. Currency conversion fees apply when selling in non-local currencies. FX spread fees of around 1.5% apply on cross-border transactions. Duties and import tax collection at checkout is available on Advanced and Plus only.

**What are the staff account limits?**
Basic allows two, Grow allows five, Advanced allows 15, and Plus is unlimited. You cannot exceed the limit without upgrading — Shopify blocks it at the admin level.

**Is Shopify Plus worth the cost?**
Only if the revenue justifies $2,300/month. A general benchmark: once you're above $500,000/year in revenue and hitting the ceiling of Advanced plan features — multi-store needs, custom checkout, automation — Plus starts making sense.
