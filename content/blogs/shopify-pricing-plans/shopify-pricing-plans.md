# Shopify Pricing Plans 2026: Which Plan Is Right for You?

Picking a Shopify plan is one of the first real decisions you make as a store owner. Get it wrong and you overpay for features you do not use. Get it right and you have a solid foundation to grow from.

The good news is Shopify has a plan for every stage, from $5 a month for first-time sellers to enterprise pricing for high-volume businesses. There is also a 3-day free trial, followed by $1 a month for your first three months.

This guide breaks down every Shopify pricing plan, what you actually pay including hidden fees, and how to choose the right plan for your business.

## Shopify Pricing Plans: A Detailed Breakdown

### I. Starter Plan: Easiest Way to Sell on Social Media

**Pricing:**
- Monthly: $5
- Online card rate: 5% + 30¢
- In-person: 5%
- International cards: extra 1%

**What you get:**
- Unlimited product listings
- Shopping cart page
- Link-in-bio tool for social selling
- Shopify Inbox for customer messages
- Basic contact page
- Product storage and fulfillment

The Starter plan does not give you a full standalone online store. It is built for selling through social media, messaging apps, and existing websites.

**Best for:** Content creators, hobbyists, and new sellers testing an idea before committing to a full store. If you plan to grow beyond social selling, you will outgrow this plan quickly.

---

### II. Basic Plan: The Most Cost-Effective Starting Point

**Pricing:**
- Monthly: ~$25
- Annual billing: $19/month
- Online card rate: 2.9% + 30¢
- In-person: 2.6% + 10¢
- Third-party payment fee: 2%

Using Shopify Payments removes the 2% third-party fee entirely. If you use an external gateway like PayPal or Stripe on a $100 sale, you pay $2 to Shopify plus the gateway's own processing fees on top.

**Best for:** Freelancers, handmade goods sellers, home-based businesses, and side projects. This is the right starting point for anyone building their first online store.

## Which Shopify Plan Fits Your Business Model?

Not every business scales the same way. Here is how to think about plan selection based on how you sell.

**Print-on-demand (POD)**
Start on Basic. Your margins are thin and order volume is unpredictable early on. Once you hit consistent monthly sales, compare your transaction fee savings on Grow vs. the higher monthly cost.

**Dropshipping**
Start on Basic. Most dropshipping operations run lean on apps and do not need multiple staff accounts early. Upgrade to Grow when your volume makes the lower transaction fees worth it.

**High-ticket DTC (furniture, jewelry, equipment)**
Consider starting on Grow or Advanced. Fewer transactions, higher order values. The lower transaction fee percentage on Advanced saves meaningful money on each sale. On a $1,000 sale, the difference between Basic (2.9%) and Advanced (2.5%) is $4 per order.

**Omnichannel (retail + online)**
Grow or Advanced, depending on staff size. You need in-person POS support, multiple staff accounts, and potentially carrier-calculated shipping for accurate fulfillment costs.

## When Should You Upgrade Your Plan?

Upgrading your plan costs more per month but saves you on transaction fees. The question is: at what sales volume does the upgrade actually pay for itself?

Here is the break-even math using Shopify Payments:

**Basic to Grow**
- Basic card rate: 2.9% + 30¢
- Grow card rate: 2.7% + 30¢
- Difference: 0.2% per transaction
- Extra monthly cost: ~$40 (monthly billing) or ~$30 (annual)

**Grow to Advanced**
- Grow card rate: 2.7% + 30¢
- Advanced card rate: 2.5% + 30¢
- Difference: 0.2% per transaction
- Extra monthly cost: ~$334 (monthly) or ~$250 (annual)
- Break-even: ~$125,000–$150,000 in monthly sales

| Upgrade | Break-even Monthly Sales |
|---------|--------------------------|
| Basic to Grow | ~$15,000–$20,000 |

## Hidden Shopify Costs to Consider

Your subscription and card processing fees are just the baseline. Here are the costs that catch merchants off guard.

### Transaction Fees on Third-Party Gateways

If you use Stripe, PayPal, or any payment gateway other than Shopify Payments, Shopify charges an additional fee on every sale: 2% on Basic, 1% on Grow, 0.6% on Advanced, and 0.15% on Plus. Using Shopify Payments removes this fee entirely.

### Shopify Tax Fee

If you use Shopify Tax to handle US sales tax, Shopify charges 0.35% of revenue processed through Shopify Tax once you exceed $100,000 in annual US sales. The fee drops to 0.25% on Shopify Plus. This is capped and only applies to US revenue, but it is a real cost that most pricing guides do not mention.

### Apps and Plugin Fees

Most stores run 5 to 15 apps. Paid apps for email marketing, reviews, loyalty, and upsells typically run $10 to $50 per month each. Tools like [Joy Loyalty](https://avada.io), [Air Reviews](https://avada.io), [Chatty](https://avada.io), and [AOV](https://avada.io) from Avada cover loyalty, reviews, live chat, and upsells in one ecosystem, which is worth evaluating before paying for multiple separate tools.

### Currency Conversion and FX Spread

If you sell internationally in local currencies, Shopify charges a currency conversion fee of 1.5% per transaction. On a $200 international order, that is $3 per sale that does not appear in your subscription cost.

### Chargeback Fees

Every disputed transaction that results in a chargeback costs $15 to $25 per case, regardless of outcome. High chargeback rates also put your Shopify Payments account at risk.

### Premium Themes

Shopify's free themes are functional for most stores. Paid themes from the official store run $150 to $380 as a one-time purchase. Third-party options on ThemeForest start around $50.

### Shipping Labels

Shopify offers discounted rates through USPS, UPS, and DHL. Budget $10 to $200+ per month depending on order volume.

## My Final Verdict: Which Shopify Plan to Choose?

For most new store owners, start on Basic. It gives you a full online store, unlimited products, and everything you need to validate your business without overpaying.

Move to Grow when your monthly sales consistently hit $15,000 to $20,000 and you need the lower transaction fees or additional staff accounts. The math works in your favor above that threshold.

Consider Advanced when you are doing $125,000 or more per month, need 15 staff accounts, or require carrier-calculated shipping and advanced market tools.

Plus is for established high-volume businesses that need enterprise infrastructure, multi-store management, and custom checkout.

## 5 Ways to Lower Your Shopify Costs

### 1. Use Shopify Payments

This removes the 0.6% to 2% third-party transaction fee on every sale. It is the single biggest lever you have on your processing costs and is available in most major markets.

### 2. Pay Annually

Annual billing saves 25% compared to monthly on Basic, Grow, and Advanced. If you are confident in the platform after your trial, switching to annual billing is straightforward cost reduction.

### 3. Buy Your Domain Externally

Shopify charges around $14 to $20 per year for a .com domain. External registrars often offer lower prices, especially in the first year with promotional rates.

### 4. Audit Your App Stack Quarterly

App fees accumulate fast. Every three months, review which apps you are actively using. Remove anything not generating measurable value. Cutting four $20/month apps saves $960 per year.

### 5. Match Your Plan to Your Current Volume

Do not pay for Advanced when Basic covers your needs. And do not stay on Basic when your monthly sales have crossed the Grow break-even threshold. Run the math quarterly and adjust accordingly.

## Shopify Pricing Plans: FAQs

**How do you avoid Shopify transaction fees?**
Use Shopify Payments as your payment gateway. This removes the additional transaction fee (2% on Basic, 1% on Grow, 0.6% on Advanced) entirely. You still pay the standard card processing rate, but the Shopify surcharge disappears.

**Is Shopify worth it for beginners?**
Yes. Shopify's setup is straightforward, its support is accessible, and the Basic plan includes everything you need to launch and run a store. The 3-day free trial plus the $1/month introductory offer gives you time to test it before committing.

**Did Shopify raise its prices?**
Shopify restructured its pricing in 2023, increasing rates on several plans. Since then, pricing has been updated again. Always check shopify.com/pricing for the most current USD figures before choosing a plan.

**Is Shopify $1 for 3 months?**
Yes. After the 3-day free trial, new merchants can access plans at $1 per month for the first three months. Check shopify.com for current promotional availability.

**What is the difference between Basic and Grow?**
Basic supports one staff account and basic reports. Grow supports five staff accounts, advanced reports, and lower card processing rates. Grow becomes cost-effective once your monthly sales consistently exceed $15,000–$20,000.

**Is Shopify expensive for small businesses?**
The Basic plan at ~$25/month is accessible for most small businesses. Costs rise with paid apps and sales volume, but the infrastructure Shopify provides — hosting, checkout, payments, security — would cost significantly more to build and maintain independently.

**Is there a free Shopify plan?**
No. Shopify does not offer a permanent free plan. The 3-day free trial and the $1/month introductory offer are the best options for testing the platform before paying full price.

Shopify pricing is straightforward once you look past the headline subscription fee. For most new stores, Basic at ~$25/month (or $19/month annually) is the right starting point.
